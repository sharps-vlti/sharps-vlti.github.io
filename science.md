The VLTI is a unique discovery machine for exploring the Universe at the Highest Angular Resolution,
by combining milli-arcsecond resolution with high sensitivity. However, the spectral capabilities
of the workhorse instruments such as GRAVITY and MATISSE have been limited to wavelength >= 2.0 micron, 
which has so far prevented access to important diagnostic spectral lines and also limited the 
inner working angle that has been accessible for high-contrast observations with VLTI.

SHARPS will enable exciting new science in the following areas:

### Exoplanets: 
High-contrast dual-field capability will allow spectroscopy of giant and rocky planets in the 30 mas–1″ range, including systems from GAIA DR4 and RV surveys. The unprecedented astrometric precision of order ten microarcsecond will enable the search for closer-in yet undetected planets or binary exoplanets/exomoons, exceeding GRAVITY’s astrometric precision and inner working angle. The instrument will access highly complementary spectral features to the existing VLTI facility instruments, including novel molecular tracers for exoplanet atmospheric retrieval.

### Planet Formation and Star Formation: 
In the field of accretion and mass ejection, the instrument will cover unique accretion- and outflow-tracing lines will allow reconstructing the 3-dimensional velocity field and the magnetic field topology in the mass accretion/ejection region around young stars and around forming planets. Obtaining precision orbits and measuring the spin-orbit alignment for large samples of binary and exoplanet systems will provide crucial constraints on the dynamical processes that shape the architecture of star and planetary systems. 

### Stellar Astrophysics: 
- Shorter wavelengths, combined with vibration control at the VLTI, will provide improved astrometric precision enabling the detection of exomoons, searches for intermediate-mass black holes, and resolved microlensing studies probing the lower mass gap between neutron stars and black holes.

### Extrgalactic Astrophysics and Cosmology: 
In extragalactic science, the instrument will be able to resolve the broadline region for an unprecedented sample of active galactic nuclei (AGNs). Together with a dedicated reverberation-mapping campaign, this should provide tight constraints on the Hubble constants and allow solving the ongoing debate on the Hubble tensions.
