To follow up on the productive discussions on science cases for SHARPS at the [VLTI Community Days in May](https://www.eso.org/sci/meetings/2026/vlti-community-days-2026.html), we plan to organise a brief science-focussed workshop:

<b>Workshop:</b> "Short-wavelength science with VLTI: planning for SHARPS”<br>
<b>Date:</b> Wednesday, July 29th<br>
<b>Location:</b> University of Exeter, UK<br>

The aim of the workshop will be to engage the community in defining the science cases for the SHARPS facility instrument. Following some introductory talks, we will discuss the most promising science cases for Y/J/H-band interferometry and work hands-on on the SHARPS science whitepaper, covering science areas around exoplanets, star/planet formation, stellar astrophysics, and extragalactic science. Depending on the interests of the participants, we are also happy to include talks on relevant Y/J/H-band activities at VLTI (e.g. Asgard/BIFROST visitor instrument and PLANETES TecDev) or to advise participants on the process for submitting exploratory P118 Asgard proposals.

The July 29 date has been chosen to coincide with the ["Discs on the Exe" conference](https://discsontheexe.org) that will bring 260+ experts on protoplanetary discs, debris discs, and exoplanets to Exeter. By scheduling the SHARPS meeting in parallel, we hope to capture new ideas from the walk-in audience and to trigger interest for the project in the broader community.

In case you are interested to attend or contribute to the SHARPS workshop please complete the following form:

[Short-wavelength science with VLTI: planning for SHARPS](https://forms.gle/uzMAFYCoH2XTb4TT9)

We note that registration for the “Discs on the Exe” conference is not mandatory to attend the SHARPS workshop. We also plan to enable remote participation via Zoom. We have a workshop budget (although very modest); please leave a comment in the registration form, in case some small financial travel support might allow you to participate.
