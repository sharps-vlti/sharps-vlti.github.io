--- layout: page --- {{ content }} SHARPS is a concept for a 3rd-generation VLTI instrument that will be proposed for ESO's 'VLT beyond 2030' call. It will enable unique science prospects from exomoons and exoplanets to intermediate-mass black holes, and cosmology, by offering the following unique characteristics:

Shorter Wavelengths (YJH-band):
Observing down to 1.0 micron will enable SHARPS to observe at 2-times higher angular resolution that possible so far, but also provide access to unique molecular atmospheric tracers and diagnostic lines of accretion and ejection.

Higher Sensitivity and higher contrast at smaller inner working angles (up to 10<-7 at 0.03"):
Dual-field capability with phase referencing will enable micro-arcsecond astrometry, while enabling the high sensitivity that is needed to study objects in the low-metallicity environment of the LMC or reflected-light exoplanets at unprecedented small inner working angle.

Higher Spectral Resolution (up to R=30,000):
Combining high spectral resolution with high angular resolution will enable detailed studies of gas kinematics, providing unique constraints for atmospheric retrieval of exoplanets or on the gas kinematics in the broad-line region of AGNs and the circumplanetary discs around forming planets.
